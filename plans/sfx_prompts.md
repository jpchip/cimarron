# Sound Effects Generation Prompts — Cimarron Visual Novel

> Prompts for ElevenLabs sound effect generation. Each entry includes the target filename,
> the scene context, emotional register, and the generation prompt.
>
> Overall palette: raw frontier Oklahoma 1889–1931. Nothing polished or cinematic-modern.
> Wood, leather, clay, canvas, iron, wind. Sounds earned by hard use.

---

## Chapter 1: The Land of the Fair God (1889)

---

### `sfx/crash_china.ogg`
**Scene 1 — Venable Dinner / Isaiah falls into the cake**
**Priority:** High | **Duration:** ~2s | **Looping:** No

> Sudden heavy crash of a large man stumbling backward into a table, followed immediately by the sharp shattering of china and porcelain dishware scattering across a hardwood floor. The impact is first — a solid thud of body weight — then the cascade of breaking dishes. Dining room acoustics. No music. The room goes silent immediately after.

**Scene 1 — Dinner table ambience**

> Quiet formal dinner table ambience. Light occasional clink of silver cutlery on fine china plates. Gentle sounds of a prosperous 1880s parlor dinner — the scrape of a fork, a glass being set down softly, the shuffle of someone leaning forward. Warm, intimate. No voices. Low murmur only.

### `sfx/cannon_start.ogg`
**Scene 2 — Land Run noon signal**
**Priority:** High | **Duration:** ~3s | **Looping:** No

> A single deep cannon shot fired outdoors. The boom rolls across open flat Oklahoma prairie — a massive, resonant concussion followed by the slow decay of the sound across empty land and sky. 1889 black powder cannon. The report itself is heavy and low, not sharp. The reverb fades slowly into silence. This is the starting gun for 50,000 people's dreams.

### `sfx/hooves_stampede.ogg`

Dust, shouting, wheels, chaos. The ground itself seems to shake. Crescendo from near-silence to full overwhelming cacophony over 6 seconds, then sustains. Not orderly — desperate, wild, human ambition made audible. 1889 Oklahoma Land Run.

A murmur that begins in surprise, grows for two seconds, then someone's voice rises, and the sound shifts from shock to something harder to name — the divided sound of a room where some are relieved and some are not. They ring with the slightly imprecise joy of ordinary people celebrating something real. Under the bells, distant crowd voices, cheering. ## Notes on Generation Settings

**For ElevenLabs generation, suggested parameters:**

| Sound Type | Duration | Prompt Influence |
|---|---|---|
| One-shot impacts (gunshot, gavel, crash) | 2–4s | High (0.8–1.0) |
| Ambient loops (press, wind, campfire) | 15–20s | Medium (0.5–0.7) |
| Character moments (last breath, baby cry) | 3–6s | High (0.9–1.0) |
| UI sounds (type clink, coin jingle) | 0.5–1.5s | High (0.8–1.0) |
| Action sequences (wagon runaway, blowout) | 6–10s | High (0.85–1.0) |

**Looping sounds** (mark as `play sfx` in Ren'Py rather than `play sound`):
- `wagon_creak.ogg`
- `campfire_crackle.ogg`
- `wind_howl.ogg`
- `printing_press.ogg`
- `oil_drill.ogg`
- `silverware_clink.ogg`
- `crowd_chaos.ogg`

**One-shot sounds** (mark as `play sound`):
- Everything else in this list.
