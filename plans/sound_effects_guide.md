# Sound Effects Guide — Cimarron Visual Novel

> **Status:** No `play sound` / `play sfx` calls currently exist in any script. All audio is music-only. This document maps every narrative moment that would benefit from a sound effect, organized by chapter and priority.

---

## Priority Tiers

- **[H]** High — dramatically crucial, player will notice the absence
- **[M]** Medium — meaningful atmospheric enhancement
- **[L]** Low — subtle polish

## Suggested Filename Conventions

Sound effects should use the `play sound` channel (one-shot, non-looping) or `play sfx` if a looping ambient layer is needed alongside music.

```renpy
play sound "sfx/gunshot.ogg"
play sound "sfx/horse_hooves.ogg"
play sound "sfx/printing_press_start.ogg"
play sound "sfx/baby_cry.ogg"
play sound "sfx/crowd_exhale.ogg"
```

## Implementation Note

The oil blowout (Scene 27), baby cry (Scene 11), Land Run cannon (Scene 1/2), and Yancey's last breath are the four moments most likely to land emotionally flat without audio. Prioritize those first.

For minigames, even simple UI sounds (click, confirm, reject) will significantly improve feel. A single `type_clink.ogg`, reused at low volume across the typesetting, trial, and letter minigames, would cover most of the need.
